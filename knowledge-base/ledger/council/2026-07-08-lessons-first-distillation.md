## Chairman's synthesis

The decision is revise: Guardian approved, no seat rejected, and five seats returned revise, so the charter sends the lessons back to the generator for one bounce before they return to you. This is worth saying plainly: not one seat found the distillation dishonest. All six verified the ledger restatements they checked — the invitations lesson, the never-test encoding, the social-gating encoding, both Guardian exemplars, and every calibration entry a seat examined came back accurate. What the five revises share is a different worry: not what the pages say, but what a compliant generator could get away with while obeying them. These pages are binding steers for future runs, and the seats read them the way a generator will — literally, and looking for the gaps.

The strongest council signal is a genuine independent convergence: Tone and Lap, from different constitutions, found the same hole. The cycle's adopted describe-don't-judge cue style — visible in the merged artifact and named in seat-calibration.md itself — never became a lesson. The cue lesson mandates a cue's presence and a serve-and-return pause but says nothing about cue content, so a generator could ship 'good girl!' with a dutiful pause and pass every bullet. Two seats walked separate paths to that exact door. It should be closed with a CONFIRMED cue-content bullet. A second, softer convergence: Guardian (as a note) and Lap (as a revise) both flagged that the forward-age-bands lesson carries the license half of the Guardian's own exemplar without its obligation half — content must still fit its own declared band and honor the adult-stance framing. Guardian didn't hold because COUNCIL.md carries the full qualifier; Lap held because the lessons page is what a generator will actually read. That severity split is the cycle's only real disagreement between seats, and Lap has the better of it for a page whose header says 'treat CONFIRMED bullets as binding steers.'

The remaining three revises are one family: n=1 rulings drifting into settled-fact wording. Explorer caught 'consistently adopts' — a trend word on a page that declares itself first-readings-only — and wants a sufficiency caveat so invitation-framing isn't read as the whole of open-endedness. Skills caught bullet 4 compressing the constitution wrongly on two load-bearing points: the KB does not define critical thinking as cause-and-effect prediction, and the counting-to-connections retag is owner-ratified precedent from one cycle, not a definitional fact. Voice caught the unscoped app-voice allowance — the ruling permitted one mechanic's count-words, but as written it could license synthetic speech into language-teaching territory that bilingual-early-years.md explicitly forbids. Lap adds one constructive gap: the cycle confirmed a practicality baseline (about five short rounds, the 9pm shape) that merged unchanged and is captured nowhere, so generators get faults but no rhythm steer.

All of this is one pass of wording surgery: two new bullets, a handful of scope guards and provenance markers, one supplement-not-replace preamble line. Guardian's clean approve on the hard bounds — no names anywhere, every lesson steering toward the constraints rather than away — means nothing here escalates. One bounce, then it comes to you.

**Key disagreements:**
- Strongest independent convergence: Tone and Lap separately found the adopted describe-don't-judge cue CONTENT style is not encoded — the cue lesson binds presence and pause only, so evaluative praise would pass every bullet.
- Guardian and Lap converged on the forward-bands lesson carrying only half its exemplar (license without the band-fit and adult-stance qualifier) — but split on severity: Guardian a non-blocking note, Lap a revise; the only true seat-vs-seat disagreement this cycle.

- Explorer's necessary-not-sufficient caveat and Tone's supplement-not-replace preamble are the same structural worry from different seats: the lessons page risks being read as complete guidance, letting un-exercised principles drop.
- Guardian's approve does not conflict with the five revises — its gate is privacy, bounds, and fidelity of the ledger restatements, all of which passed for every seat that checked; the revises live entirely in generator-facing scope and completeness.
- Lap alone flagged the missing practicality baseline (about five short rounds, the 9pm shape) — no convergence, but no seat contradicts it either; it is the only purely additive finding.
- Skills' definitional finding (critical thinking is 'the ongoing search for reliable knowledge,' not cause-and-effect prediction) is a factual correction to the artifact, uncontested by any other seat.
