Distilled from the ledger's first full cycle (the count-game proposal, its
Council escalation, and the owner's revise-then-merge ruling). Written for
the next Gardener or Mechanic run: treat CONFIRMED bullets as binding steers.

- **CONFIRMED — Prompts are invitations, never quizzes.** Any prompt built
  around a question with one right answer reads as testing the little one;
  5 of 6 seats independently converged on this fault and the owner adopted a
  reframe to a shared do-it-together invitation
  (knowledge-base/ledger/council/2026-07-08-count-with-me.md;
  knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). Check every prompt line
  before proposing: no right-answer question, no implied score.

- **CONFIRMED — Every round needs a grown-up cue with a serve-and-return
  pause.** The count game shipped with no cue at all, so the prompt spoke
  straight to the child and positioned the app as the one conducting the
  activity; the owner adopted per-round cues (added to schema, renderer, and
  all rounds) where the grown-up voices the activity and the pause is the
  little one's turn (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md).
  New mechanics must include cue support from the first draft.

- **CONFIRMED — The app is support, never the teacher.** Owner ruling on the
  Voice Seat's strongest form: the app may keep its gentle spoken labels and
  count-words, but the grown-up must be the primary voice; never write copy
  where the app conducts the exchange
  (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md).

- **CONFIRMED — Skill tags must be honest against seven-life-skills.md.**
  Counting a small set exercises one-to-one correspondence — a connections
  skill — not the cause-and-effect prediction the KB defines as critical
  thinking; the owner adopted the retag
  (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). Tag only what the
  mechanic genuinely exercises; when reaching for a second tag, reread the
  skill definitions first.

- **CONFIRMED — Forward age-bands are in scope; do not self-censor them.**
  The owner ruled that the founding fact fixes where the little one is
  today while the schema and roadmap deliberately plan forward; forward-band
  content earns a Council note, not a hold
  (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md).

- **CONFIRMED — New mechanics are class decisions; escalation is the normal
  path, not failure.** The count mechanic was approved as a class by the
  owner's merge itself; the Council's job on a new mechanic is an advisory
  pre-read (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). Propose the
  class cleanly and expect the owner, not the Council, to clear it.

- **HYPOTHESIS — Make no-fail behavior legible in the artifact itself.**
  Two seats inferred a fail-state from a fixed target count, but the runtime
  made a wrong count impossible; the Council reviews the YAML, not the
  renderer (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). State the
  no-fail behavior in the proposal (e.g., repeats are gentle no-ops) so
  reviewers can verify it without reading code.

- **HYPOTHESIS — Fix confirmed findings in the same cycle.** The
  neutral-referent sweep landed after the count-game review ran, so the
  council record at knowledge-base/ledger/council/2026-07-08-count-with-me.md
  still preserves pre-sweep phrasing inside its quoted principle lines (see
  log.md, 2026-07-08). Delay lets a confirmed fault propagate into new
  artifacts and permanent records; paraphrase, never quote, when citing
  older ledger events.
