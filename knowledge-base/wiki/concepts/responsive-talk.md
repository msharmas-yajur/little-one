# Responsive Talk

How the grown-up *talks* during the shared learning experience — synthesized from
three books. This directly shapes the site's grown-up `cue:` lines and the
back-and-forth Months (6–9). All of it is in service of the little one's turn.

## The toolkit
- **Serve and return.** Treat every babble, point, look, and gesture as a
  conversational turn; answer it, then wait. (*Scientist in the Crib*, *Mind in the
  Making*.) → the engine behind Month 6's "pause."
- **Parentese on purpose.** The warm, slow, sing-song voice helps the little one parse words —
  a feature of reading aloud, not baby-talk to outgrow. (*Scientist in the Crib*.)
- **Acknowledge feelings first.** Name the feeling before redirecting: "You're
  cross the tower fell." She reads tone and face before words. (*How to Talk*.)
- **Describe, don't judge.** "You turned the page!" beats "good girl." Descriptive
  narration feeds vocabulary and shows the little one their actions matter. (*How to Talk*.) → the
  house style for all `cue:` lines.
- **Give information in a word.** One clear label + gesture ("gentle," stroking the
  page) instead of a lecture. (*How to Talk*.)
- **Offer a real choice.** Two options, honor the little one's reach — early agency. (*How to
  Talk*.) → Month 9.
- **Playfulness.** Silly voices, animal sounds, peekaboo — play is the little one's native
  channel and keeps the game forgiving. (*How to Talk*.)

## Design implications for the site
- **`cue:` lines** should model *descriptive* talk and *serve-and-return* pauses —
  a good cue tells the grown-up what to say **and** to wait for the little one's turn.
- Keep cues warm and non-corrective (never "test" the little one) — matches the forgiving-game
  and lap-activity design rules.
- Candidate future idea: a per-page "wait a beat" nudge in the player that
  encourages the grown-up to pause for the little one's turn (would need a `raw/ideas/` note
  first).

## Caveat
*How to Talk* targets ages 2–7; the moves above are adapted down to ~12 months —
tone, gesture, and narration over verbal scripts. General guidance, not personalized
advice.
