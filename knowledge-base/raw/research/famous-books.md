# Research note — famous books for 1–2 year olds, across the world

Reference for what's beloved in this age group worldwide, to inform *original*
story ideas for the site. **We never reproduce these books' text or art** — they
are copyrighted. This note captures what makes them work and what themes to draw
on, so we write our own in the same spirit.

Assume the founding fact: the little one is 1 year old today.

## What "famous at 1–2" has in common
Durable board books; rhythm and repetition; bright or bold-simple pictures;
something to touch, lift, or do; short enough to reread endlessly. Story matters
less than pattern, naming, and sound. These are exactly the site's principles.

## The global classics (loved almost everywhere)
- Eric Carle — hungry-caterpillar eating sequence with finger-holes; the
  "what do you see?" repeating-animal pattern (20M+ copies); a "can you do
  this?" copy-the-animal movement book.
- Sandra Boynton — silly animal-sound and bedtime romps, very rhythmic.
- Bill Martin Jr. — bouncy alphabet-on-a-tree chant.
- Margaret Wise Brown — the quiet "say goodnight to each thing" bedtime book.
- Peggy Rathmann — wordless-ish "animals sneak out of the zoo" bedtime caper.
- Little-blue-truck style friendly-vehicle rhymes.
- Pat-the-Bunny style touch-and-feel first books (11M+ copies).

## United Kingdom
- Rod Campbell — lift-the-flap "the zoo sends the wrong pet" (since 1980).
- Fiona Watt / Usborne — the "That's Not My…" touch-and-feel series (huge at 1).
- Lucy Cousins — the Maisy mouse series (bold outlines, since early 1990s).
- Sam McBratney — the "guess how much I love you" hare bedtime classic.
- Julia Donaldson — rhyming tales (a touch old at 1, adored nearing 2).

## Japan (minimalist, high craft)
- Taro Gomi — clean, simple design with emotional weight; a "spot the difference
  among the same animals" hiding book; a runaway-goldfish seek-and-find.
- Kazuo Iwamura — gentle animal-family stories.
- Register: understated, soft, uncluttered rather than loud-bright.

## India (especially relevant here — bilingual first books)
- Publishers: Tulika, Pratham Books (StoryWeaver has free early titles in dozens
  of Indian languages), Karadi Tales.
- Themes rooted in Indian life: animals & their sounds, festivals, food, family,
  everyday city/village scenes; often bilingual/multilingual.
- Barefoot Books "Our World" series (e.g. an India title) pairs scenes with
  English + Hindi and pronunciation help.
- Value at this age: hearing a home language alongside English. Worth building
  toward on the site.

## Interactive/novelty loved worldwide
- Ingela P. Arrhenius (Sweden) — peekaboo slider books with a mirror ending.
- Lift-the-flap (Karen Katz style); "indestructible" chew-proof books for the
  very youngest.

## Choosing by age
- At 1: sturdiest board + touch/flap; rhythm & repetition over plot; naming and
  sounds.
- Toward 2: a little more story; counting; simple problem-solving.

## Original story-theme ideas for the site (drawn from the above, not copied)
These are *directions*, to be specced as `wiki/features/` stories if pursued.
Each stays within the site's design principles (lap activity; original art;
tap-and-name; a grown-up cue per page).

- **A repeating "what do you see?" pattern** — one animal "sees" the next; big
  tappable art; predictable so she anticipates. (Pattern, not text, borrowed.)
- **A copy-the-movement page** — "can you clap? can you stomp?"; the grown-up
  does it with her. Ties to Month 4 (imitation) and Month 3 (cause/effect).
- **A hide-and-find spread** — the same friendly animals with one hiding; taps
  reveal. Ties to lantern attention (Month 2) and object permanence.
- **A gentle "goodnight to each thing" bedtime story** — say goodnight to the
  sun, the birds, the moon; whisper register. (We already have a version.)
- **A bilingual first-words idea (India-specific)** — name a few things in
  English and a home language on the same page (e.g. "dog / kutta"), with a
  grown-up cue to say both. Would need a small data change to support two labels;
  spec before building. Strong developmental value; highest-priority new idea.
- **Animal sounds on tap** — real "woof/quack/moo" instead of soft chimes for
  animal art. Backlog item already noted on the storybook-player feature page.
