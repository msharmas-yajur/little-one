# Book note — Mind in the Making

> Raw source. Faithful summary from web research (see Sources). No names; general
> science only.

## Bibliographic
- **Title:** *Mind in the Making: The Seven Essential Life Skills Every Child Needs*.
- **Author:** Ellen Galinsky (Families and Work Institute).
- **First published:** 2010 (HarperStudio / HarperCollins).

## Core thesis
The capacities children most need are not early academics but seven
executive-function-rooted **life skills**. These are not picked up automatically —
they are fostered through everyday, responsive adult–child interaction. Galinsky
pairs "what the research shows" (100+ researchers interviewed) with concrete
things a grown-up can do today.

## The Seven Essential Life Skills (with a ~12-month application)
1. **Focus and Self-Control** — attention, remembering, resisting distraction.
   → Follow her gaze, name what she's looking at, let one page hold her attention.
2. **Perspective Taking** — figuring out what others think and feel.
   → Narrate feelings out loud and mirror her expressions.
3. **Communicating** — conveying and listening through words, gesture, expression.
   → "Serve and return": respond to every babble, point, and gesture as a turn.
4. **Making Connections** — sorting, seeing same/different, linking new to known.
   → Point out same/different; link book to world ("a dog — like our dog!").
5. **Critical Thinking** — the ongoing search for reliable knowledge.
   → Cause-and-effect play (peekaboo, drop-and-retrieve, lift-the-flap); pause and
     let her anticipate what happens next.
6. **Taking on Challenges** — willingness to try hard things and persist.
   → Offer a "just-right" challenge; let her struggle briefly before helping;
     stay a warm secure base so trying feels safe.
7. **Self-Directed, Engaged Learning** — curiosity and motivation to keep learning.
   → Follow her lead — let her choose the book, turn pages, set the pace.

## Other key ideas
- **Executive function is the backbone.** The seven skills translate EF (working
  memory, self-control, mental flexibility) into everyday language; EF is buildable
  at any age.
- **Serve-and-return + the "Still Face."** Responsive, contingent back-and-forth is
  central; Tronick's *Still Face* shows how much a baby depends on the adult's face,
  tone, and responsiveness — validating the engaged-grown-up premise of a lap
  activity.
