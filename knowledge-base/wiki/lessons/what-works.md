Distilled from the ledger's first full cycle (the count-game proposal, its
Council escalation, and the owner's revise-then-merge ruling); revised once
after the Council's review of this page itself
(knowledge-base/ledger/council/2026-07-08-lessons-first-distillation.md).
Written for the next Gardener or Mechanic run: treat CONFIRMED bullets as
binding steers. These lessons SUPPLEMENT the constitutions in wiki/concepts/
and the charters — they never replace them; a principle no cycle has
exercised yet still binds in full.

- **CONFIRMED — Prompts are invitations, never quizzes.** Any prompt built
  around a question with one right answer reads as testing the little one;
  5 of 6 seats independently converged on this fault and the owner adopted a
  reframe to a shared do-it-together invitation
  (knowledge-base/ledger/council/2026-07-08-count-with-me.md;
  knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). Check every prompt line
  before proposing: no right-answer question, no implied score. Necessary,
  not sufficient: an invitation can still be a closed, adult-conducted
  activity — open-endedness and child-led focus are judged per artifact, and
  the Explorer suggestions the ruling did not adopt remain open questions,
  not settled steers.

- **CONFIRMED — Every round needs a grown-up cue with a serve-and-return
  pause.** The count game shipped with no cue at all, so the prompt spoke
  straight to the child and positioned the app as the one conducting the
  activity; the owner adopted per-round cues (added to schema, renderer, and
  all rounds) where the grown-up voices the activity and the pause is the
  little one's turn (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md).
  New mechanics must include cue support from the first draft.

- **CONFIRMED — Cue content is descriptive, never evaluative.** A cue must
  report what the little one actually does ("you tapped an apple!"), never
  hand down verdict-praise ("well done!" alone). The house style merged with
  the count game itself — see the apple-round cue in `_data/wordgames.yml`,
  adopted via knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md — and two
  seats independently converged on binding it here
  (knowledge-base/ledger/council/2026-07-08-lessons-first-distillation.md).
  A cue with a dutiful pause that still judges or tests fails this lesson.

- **CONFIRMED — The app is support, never the teacher.** Owner ruling on the
  Voice Seat's strongest form: the app may keep gentle in-mechanic labels
  and count-words, but the grown-up must be the primary voice; never write
  copy where the app conducts the exchange
  (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). Scope guard: that
  allowance covered one mechanic's count-words — it is NOT a license for
  language-teaching pronunciation. Bilingual words belong to the grown-up,
  never a synthetic voice (raw/research/bilingual-early-years.md).

- **CONFIRMED — Skill tags must be honest against seven-life-skills.md.**
  The KB defines Critical Thinking as the ongoing search for reliable
  knowledge; cause-and-effect play is this band's application of it, not the
  definition. Counting a small set exercises one-to-one correspondence, and
  the owner adopted its retag to connections
  (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md) — a RULING-DERIVED
  mapping (the constitution is silent on counting), not a definitional fact.
  Tag only what the mechanic genuinely exercises, and reread the seven
  definitions before tagging any new numeracy-adjacent mechanic.

- **CONFIRMED — Forward age-bands are in scope; declare the band and fit
  it.** The owner ruled that the founding fact fixes where the little one is
  today while the schema and roadmap deliberately plan forward; forward-band
  content earns a Council note, not a hold
  (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). The obligation half:
  declare the band, make the content genuinely fit its OWN declared band,
  and keep the adult-stance framing — the arc stays a practice for the
  grown-up, never a curriculum for the child.

- **CONFIRMED — New mechanics are class decisions; escalation is the normal
  path, not failure.** The count mechanic was approved as a class by the
  owner's merge itself; the Council's job on a new mechanic is an advisory
  pre-read (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). Propose the
  class cleanly and expect the owner, not the Council, to clear it.

- **HYPOTHESIS — Make no-fail behavior legible in the artifact itself.**
  Two seats inferred a fail-state from a fixed target count, but the runtime
  made a wrong count impossible; the Council reviews the YAML, not the
  renderer (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md). State the
  no-fail behavior in the proposal (e.g., repeats are gentle no-ops) so
  reviewers can verify it without reading code.

- **HYPOTHESIS — Practicality baseline: a shape a tired grown-up can hold
  at 9pm.** A few short rounds (the count game's five drew no seat objection
  and merged unchanged) and one concrete, big-target gesture per round
  (knowledge-base/ledger/decisions/2026-07-08-pr3.md;
  knowledge-base/ledger/council/2026-07-08-lessons-first-distillation.md).
  Length and rhythm are steers to hit, not just faults to avoid.

- **HYPOTHESIS — Fix confirmed findings in the same cycle.** The
  neutral-referent sweep landed after the count-game review ran, so the
  council record at knowledge-base/ledger/council/2026-07-08-count-with-me.md
  still preserves pre-sweep phrasing inside its quoted principle lines (see
  log.md, 2026-07-08). Delay lets a confirmed fault propagate into new
  artifacts and permanent records; paraphrase, never quote, when citing
  older ledger events.
