## Core message
The first five years shape lifelong learning, behaviour, and well-being — and the
everyday things a grown-up does (connecting, talking, playing) are what drive it.
Opens with: "What if I was to tell you that a game of peek-a-boo could change the
world?"

## The five things that matter most
Healthy early development leans on: **connecting, talking, playing, a healthy
home, and community.**

## Serve and return (the heart of it)
Positive, reciprocal **"serve and return"** interactions — a grown-up way of
saying **connect, talk, and play** — build a child's relationships, mental health,
and life skills. The guidance: **early and often.** (Same idea the KB already uses
in `wiki/concepts/responsive-talk.md`.)

## Simple games and what they build (maps to the site)
- **Copycat games** → imagination and empathy. (→ arc Month 4, imitation; the
  story hero "copy me" idea in `famous-books.md`.)
- **Naming games** → vocabulary and attention. (→ the tap-and-find game; arc
  Month 10, rich language; bilingual-first-words.)
- **Peekaboo** → memory and trust. (→ object-permanence / hide-and-find story idea
  in `famous-books.md`.)
- The talk also **demonstrates what happens when a caregiver goes unresponsive**
  (the "still-face" idea, Tronick) — underscoring how much a baby depends on a
  warm, responsive grown-up. (Cross-refs `raw/research/books/mind-in-the-making.md`.)

## How it informs the arc
A concise, parent-friendly anchor for the whole project's premise: the shared lap
activity *is* the mechanism. Reinforces Months 4 (imitation), 6 (serve-and-return
pause), 10 (naming). Good first thing to show a grown-up who wants the "why" in 5
minutes.
