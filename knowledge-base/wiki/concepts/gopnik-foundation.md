# The Gopnik Foundation

The project's developmental basis, drawn from Alison Gopnik's work
(*The Scientist in the Crib*; *The Gardener and the Carpenter*).

Core ideas the site is built on:
- **The child as scientist.** A young child runs experiments on the world
  (drop, repeat, compare). The adult's job is to let experiments run and put
  words to them, not to correct or redirect.
- **Gardener, not carpenter.** Shaping a child toward a fixed target (carpenter)
  is less effective and less honest than tending a rich environment they grow
  within (gardener). This is *why* the arc is an adult practice, not a child
  curriculum.
- **Lantern attention.** Young children attend broadly rather than narrowly.
  Following the child's focus beats imposing the adult's.
- **Learning through relationship & play.** Warm, responsive, language-rich,
  self-directed play builds the traits (persistence, curiosity, regulation)
  that predict later flourishing — not early academic drilling.

See `raw/research/gopnik-notes.md` for the fuller notes and the caution about
hothousing / early-academic approaches (early gains that fade, opportunity cost).
