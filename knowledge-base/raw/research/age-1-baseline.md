# Research note — the 1-year-old baseline (the starting point)

The founding fact: the little one is 1 year old today. Roughly what that means
developmentally (varies a lot child to child — not a checklist):

- Learns through play, repetition, and back-and-forth with trusted people.
- Explores cause and effect (drop, bang, put-in/take-out, repeat).
- Attends broadly ("lantern"), follows interest, easily delighted.
- Language: babbling, maybe a few words; understands far more than she says;
  benefits hugely from being talked to and having things named.
- Screens: major pediatric guidance discourages solo screen time before ~18
  months; video-chat is the exception. Hence the site is a *shared* lap activity.

Implication for the arc: Month 1 starts at "let her explore," and the
year builds forward from here. Advance by the real child, not the calendar.

Caveat: general picture only; not personalized developmental advice.
